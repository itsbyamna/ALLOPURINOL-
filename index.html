Overview

Allopurinol is a urate-lowering medication approved by the FDA. It is a xanthine oxidase inhibitor used for treating gout, hyperuricemia, and prevention of tumor lysis syndrome.

  • Drug Category: Xanthine oxidase inhibitor
  • Chemical Formula: 1,5-dihydro-4H-pyrazolo[3,4-d]pyrimidin-4-one
  • Molecular Weight: 136.11 g/mol
  • Dosage Forms: 100mg & 300mg tablets
  • Storage: 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F), protect from light

Drug Profile

  • Drug Category: Xanthine oxidase inhibitor
  • Chemical Formula: C5H4N4O
  • Molecular Weight: 136.11 g/mol
  • Dosage Forms: 100mg & 300mg tablets
  • Storage: 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F), protect from light

Mechanism of Action

Allopurinol is metabolized in the liver to oxypurinol (active metabolite). Both inhibit xanthine oxidase, blocking the conversion of hypoxanthine to uric acid, reducing urate levels in blood.

Indications & Dosage

  • Hyperuricemia (FDA approved): 200–300 mg orally daily
  • Gout: 100 mg orally daily (initial), titrate as needed
  • Prevention of tumor lysis syndrome: 300–800 mg orally daily in divided doses
PREGNANCY: Category C
LACTATION: Advise not to breastfeed during treatment and for 1 week after last dose
STORAGE: Store at 20–25°C (68–77°F), protect from light

Safety & Adverse Effects

  • Pregnancy: Category C
  • Lactation: Avoid breastfeeding during and 1 week after treatment
  • Common: Diarrhea, nausea, rash, gout flares
  • Severe: Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS), toxic epidermal necrolysis (TEN), vasculitis, hepatocellular injury, acute kidney injury, fever, leukocytosis, eosinophilia
  • SJS: Stevens-Johnson syndrome
  • TEN: Toxic epidermal necrolysis

Pharmacokinetics

  • Absorption: ~90% from GIT
  • Metabolism: Liver, transforms allopurinol into oxypurinol (active metabolite)
  • Excretion: Kidneys (mainly as oxypurinol)
  • Plasma Peak: 1.5h (Allopurinol), 4.5h (Oxypurinol)
  • Half-life: 1–2h (Allopurinol), 15h (Oxypurinol)

Drug Interactions

  • Azathioprine, Mercaptopurine: Increased toxicity; reduce dose of these agents by 66–75% if used with allopurinol.
  • Warfarin: Enhanced anticoagulant effect; monitor INR closely.
  • Thiazide diuretics: Increased risk of hypersensitivity.
  • Moderate: Pyrazinamide (hyperuricemia), uricosuric drugs (may decrease hypouricemic effects).
  • Other: Cyclophosphamide, ampicillin/amoxicillin (rash risk).
